## Incident
- Type: cardiac arrest (witnessed)
- Initial rhythm: ventricular fibrillation
- Bystander CPR: yes (~90s before EMS arrival)
- Address: 4218 Oak Crescent (residential)

## Personnel
- Lead: Medic Alvarez (PCP-1234)
- Driver: Medic Chen (PCP-2261)

## Patient
- 65 yo male, no allergies, takes lisinopril + atorvastatin
- Past medical: HTN, hyperlipidemia, prior MI (2018)

## Narrative

Dispatched to 4218 Oak Crescent for "unresponsive male, CPR in progress."
Arrived 02:00 from incident start. Spouse performing chest compressions,
reports witnessed collapse ~90s prior.

Patient pulseless, apneic. Initial rhythm VF on monitor. Continuous high-quality
compressions resumed at 02:15, ventilations via BVM 30:2. First defibrillation
at 200J delivered at 02:30 — *"clear, shock delivered, resume compressions."*

IV access obtained left AC at 03:45. Epinephrine 1mg IV at 04:00, second dose
at 07:30, third dose at 09:00. i-gel supraglottic airway placed at 05:30; ETCO2
36 with good waveform.

Second defibrillation at 200J at 06:00. Rhythm check at 07:30 showed organized
narrow-complex rhythm; pulse check positive at carotid. ROSC declared at 08:00.

Post-ROSC: NSR @ 92 bpm, BP 102/68, ETCO2 38, SpO2 96% on BVM. Loaded patient
at 10:30 and transported to regional cardiac receiving center, code 3.
