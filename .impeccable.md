# Calyx (Sentinel) — Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

EMS quality-improvement reviewers, QA staff, and clinical leads. They sit down post-incident and reconcile a Patient Care Report against body-cam video and dispatch audio to surface protocol deviations and produce a reviewer-ready After-Action Report. Context: focused desk work, often in offices or station break rooms, sometimes under time pressure between shifts. They are clinically literate, skeptical of automated outputs, and need to trust what they're reading before they sign off on it.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **rigorous, calm, fast.**

- **Rigorous** — every claim cites a source (timestamp, PCR sentence, video frame). The interface should *show its work*. Density of evidence is the brand.
- **Calm** — reviewers are reading about emergencies; the UI itself shouldn't add urgency. Restrained palette, generous spacing in the right places, no alarm aesthetics.
- **Fast** — fast to scan, fast to respond, fast to navigate between findings, video, and PCR. Performance and information clarity are part of the feel.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Reference touchstone:** a bit Palantir-esque — intelligence-product seriousness, monochromatic surfaces with sharp single-hue accents, dense data presented confidently, monospace as a load-bearing signal of "machine-verified" rather than decoration.

**Theme:** Light. Cream paper surface (`#F5F4F0`), nearly-white card surface (`#FAF9F5`), warm dark text (`#1A1A1A`). The cream isn't a stylistic flourish — it cuts the clinical-lab feel and gives the interface an archival/editorial quality, like a journal article rather than a dashboard. Reviewers' work is more like reading a brief than monitoring a system.

**Accent:** sky blue `#70c1ff` is the single load-bearing accent. Used sparingly for primary actions, active states, and the "machine-derived" markers (timeline pins, finding cards, reconciled events). Supporting semantic colors: sage `#3D5A3D` for confirmed/success, deep red `#B33A3A` for discrepancies/danger, warm gray `#9A9890` for muted/inactive.

**Anti-aesthetics:** not a SaaS dashboard, not a medical EHR, not a Bootstrap admin theme, not a glowing dark-mode "AI tool." No purple-blue gradients. No glassmorphism. No giant rounded cards with stock icons.

**Typography:** Inter (body) + JetBrains Mono (utility/labels) for now — both are reflex picks but kept intentionally for the hackathon. Mono carries the "data has provenance" signal — timestamps, IDs, codes, status labels all live in mono. Body sans for prose.

### Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion.** Every AI-derived statement is paired with its source. The UI should make it trivially easy to jump from a finding → its citation. If a claim can't link back to a timestamp, sentence, or frame, it doesn't belong in the UI.
2. **Mono carries weight.** Uppercase mono = machine-verified, structured, clickable provenance. Sans = human prose. This split is the typographic system; respect it everywhere.
3. **Restraint over decoration.** No accent stripes on cards, no decorative gradients, no "icon + heading + body" template tiles. When in doubt, remove.
4. **Density where it earns trust.** Reviewers want to see everything. Don't hide information behind progressive disclosure if a denser layout reads cleanly. But pace the density — tight where comparable, generous where the eye needs to land.
5. **Speed is a feature.** Optimistic UI on every interaction, instant seeking between findings/video/PCR, no spinners where a skeleton or a cached value will do.

### Constraints

- React 18 + TypeScript + Vite + Tailwind 4. Existing component library: Radix primitives, lucide-react icons, motion (framer-motion successor), recharts.
- Hackathon timeline — accessibility floor is informal (functional, not WCAG-audited). Don't introduce regressions, but don't block on a11y.
- Schema is locked: `frontend/src/types/schemas.ts` mirrors `backend/app/schemas.py`. Design freely; changes to data shape need explicit user approval.
- Two surfaces: the **landing page** (marketing — currently editorial-clinical with cream + sky-blue) and the **app** (3-pane reviewer UI). Landing has more permission for visual statement; app has more permission for density and utility.
