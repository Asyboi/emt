============================================================
NARRATIVE
============================================================

Medic 14 was assigned to CAD incident 230010011 for an unconscious person in
Manhattan, ZIP code 10002. Unit was assigned at 00:08:20, activated at 00:08:32,
and arrived on scene at 00:21:37.

Upon arrival, crew was directed inside residence by family member. Patient was
found supine on living room floor. Patient was an approximately 62-year-old male
who was unresponsive to verbal and painful stimuli. Patient was apneic and
pulseless. No obvious signs of trauma were observed. CPR was initiated
immediately.

Cardiac monitor was applied and initial rhythm was ventricular fibrillation.
Defibrillation was delivered at 200J and CPR was resumed immediately. Airway was
managed with BVM ventilation using 100% oxygen. IO access was established in the
left tibia and epinephrine 1 mg was administered. A second rhythm check showed
continued ventricular fibrillation and a second shock was delivered at 200J.
CPR was continued.

Advanced airway was placed and confirmed with chest rise and ETCO2 monitoring.
Additional epinephrine was administered per cardiac arrest protocol. Rhythm
later changed to PEA. CPR was continued. At approximately 00:39, pulse check
showed carotid pulse present. ROSC was achieved. Patient remained unconscious
and required assisted ventilations.

Patient was moved to stretcher and secured. Unit departed scene to hospital at
00:41:37 and arrived at the emergency department at 00:47:46. Care was
transferred to ED staff with verbal report given. Patient was transferred in
critical condition, post-cardiac arrest with ROSC.
